Phase 0 — BLE Reverse Engineering

Step-by-step: from unknown device to documented protocol

Step 1 — Passive scan
nRF Connect on Android

Turn on the A550. Open nRF Connect → Scanner tab → find the device. Note the advertised name and manufacturer data bytes in the advertisement packet. Connect → explore the GATT server: list all Service UUIDs and Characteristic UUIDs. Try reading each readable characteristic and note the raw byte values.

↓
Step 2 — Traffic capture
Android HCI Snoop Log

On Android: Developer Options → Enable Bluetooth HCI snoop log. Connect the A550 (with nRF Connect or any app), move probes between hot/cold to generate readings. Pull the log: adb pull /sdcard/btsnoop_hci.log (path varies by phone). Open in Wireshark → filter btatt → find notification packets on the temp characteristic.

↓
Step 3 — Decode bytes
Map raw bytes → temperature values

Observe notification payload while changing probe temp (ice water = ~0°C, boiling = ~100°C). Common patterns: 16-bit little-endian integer × 0.1 = °C, or big-endian, or fixed-point. Record service UUID, characteristic UUID, byte offsets for probe 1 and probe 2.

↓
Step 4 — Shortcut (try first)
Check online resources

Before full capture: search GitHub for "A550 bluetooth", "44-1794 BLE", "GrillSmart protocol". Also check Home Assistant community forums — thermometer integrations are often already reverse-engineered there. May save hours of work.
